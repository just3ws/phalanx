# Suits and Strategy

<section class="card">
  <h2>Suit Identity</h2>
  <p><strong>Hearts + Diamonds = Shields (defense)</strong></p>
  <p><strong>Spades + Clubs = Weapons (attack)</strong></p>
</section>

<section class="card">
  <h2>What Each Suit Means</h2>
  <div class="table-wrap">
    <table>
      <thead>
        <tr><th>Suit</th><th>Role</th><th>High-Level Effect</th></tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr><td>Diamonds</td><td>Shield</td><td>Front-line defensive amplification.</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Hearts</td><td>Shield</td><td>Reduces overflow pressure into player LP in key paths.</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Clubs</td><td>Weapon</td><td>Pressures back-row defense when damage overflows.</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Spades</td><td>Weapon</td><td>Increases direct LP threat once damage reaches player.</td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="two-col">
  <article class="card">
    <h2>Practical Shield Play</h2>
    <ul class="quick-list">
      <li>Keep resilient cards in front where defensive effects matter most.</li>
      <li>Protect columns that are likely to take repeated pressure.</li>
      <li>Treat formation as a resource, not just card count.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>

  <article class="card">
    <h2>Practical Weapon Play</h2>
    <ul class="quick-list">
      <li>Use Clubs and Spades to convert tempo into overflow value.</li>
      <li>Attack columns where overflow can chain cleanly.</li>
      <li>Force uncomfortable reinforcement decisions.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</section>
